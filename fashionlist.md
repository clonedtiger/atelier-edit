Here is the complete, consolidated list of all the recommended apps, blogs, retail edits, and video creators mentioned across our conversation.

I. Pure Visual Lookbooks & Magazines

Vogue Runway App: Free mobile app offering global runway collections and street style photography without comment feeds or social distractions.

Who What Wear (Website): Highly actionable trend breakdowns, outfit formulas, and curated editorial shopping guides.

NET-A-PORTER’s "PORTER" Magazine: Premium digital fashion editorial featuring professional styling advice and beauty routines.

II. Retail Edits (The High-Street Shortcuts)

Zara ("Campaign" & "Studio" lines): Excellent source for dramatic, sharp tailoring and architectural shapes heavily inspired by runway fashion like McQueen.

Mango ("Selection" & "Capsule" collections): Ideal for high-quality, high-structure bouclé, tweeds, and sharp shoulders mimicking classic Chanel styling.

AllSaints: The benchmark web lookbook for styling heavy leather, hardware, and an effortlessly edgy look with classic items.

Stitch Fix Style Blog: A public retail resource full of highly visual, step-by-step guides on body shape dressing and outfit configuration.

III. Wardrobe & Collage Apps

Indyx / Cladwell: Utility apps designed to catalog her own closet (or use placeholders) to receive quick, algorithmic daily styling recommendations.

Fits (Outfit Creator App): A digital playground app that strips the background away from clothes she screenshots online, letting her build her own lookbooks.

Shuffles (by Pinterest): A standalone collage tool built entirely for making quick, visual mood boards and dragging styling ideas together.

IV. Dedicated Video & Makeup Guides (On-Demand Utility)

Alyssa Beltempo (YouTube): Focuses heavily on "shopping your closet" and teaching how to introduce a creative, subversive, and edgy twist to classic clothes.

Audrey Coyne (YouTube): Specializes in timeless, elegant, and classic fashion (Chanel-coded) with simple rules on how to keep the look modern.

Lisa Eldridge (YouTube): Clean, direct, world-class makeup tutorials focused entirely on proper technique and striking, classic looks.

Sephora App (How-To Tab): Completely native, non-social video tutorials and product pairing lookbooks tailored to specific skin profiles.

V. Aggregators & Workflow Tools

Feedly: An RSS reader that automatically streams updates from her chosen blogs (like Who What Wear) into a singular, clean digital magazine view.

Apple Freeform:Apple Freeform: A completely free, infinite native canvas on Mac and iPad where she can drag and drop images, text notes, and YouTube video boxes.

Notion: A personal organization platform that allows the creation of a "Gallery View" database to hold web clippings, turning URLs into a visual grid of photos.

Apple Shortcuts (macOS Integration): The modern built-in automation replacement for Mac's Automator app. Ideal for building a custom "Quick Action" so she can right-click any image or share any Safari link directly to her Freeform style board with one tap.
